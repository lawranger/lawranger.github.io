My Name is Brandon

I've proudly dedicated over 20 years to serving the State of North Carolina, beginning my career in law enforcement as a police officer before transitioning into the field of information technology. My journey led me to the NC Department of Public Safety, where I served as a Data Center Administrator, managing critical infrastructure that supported statewide operations. Today, I continue that mission in a technical role with the NC State Highway Patrol, where I provide IT support to enhance operational readiness and public safety communications.

Throughout my career, I've remained deeply committed to public service, driven by a desire to solve problems, support those on the front lines, and ensure that technology serves as a reliable backbone for mission-critical functions. As I approach retirement, I'm looking forward to the next chapter, one that allows me to continue making a meaningful impact while embracing the opportunities that come with decades of experience in both law enforcement and IT.

Outside of work, I have a passion for adventure motorcycles. I find freedom and clarity on the open road, often leading or participating in long-distance rides across the region with fellow riders. These journeys not only reflect my love for exploration and precision but also mirror the same values I've carried throughout my career: discipline, resilience, and a constant drive to push boundaries.

Whether it's troubleshooting networks or navigating winding roads on my BMW GSA, I'm fueled by a love for challenges and the satisfaction of overcoming them. The road ahead is still wide open, and I'm ready for wherever it leads.
